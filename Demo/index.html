Multi-SpectroGAN: High-Diversity and High-Fidelity Spectrogram Generation with Adversarial Style Recombination for Speech Synthesis

BLIND

ABSTRACT

While Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) based neural text-to-speech (TTS) systems have shown significant improvement for neural speech synthesis, there is no TTS system to learn to synthesize speech from text sequence only with an adversarial feedback. Since adversarial feedback alone is not sufficient to train the generator, current models still require the reconstruction loss compared between ground-truth and generated mel-spectrogram directly. In this paper, we present Multi-SpectroGAN (MSG), which can train the multi-speaker model only with the adversarial feedback by conditioning a self-supervised hidden representation of generator to conditional discriminator. This leads to better guidance on generator training. In addition, we also propose the adversarial style recombination (ASR) for better generalization in unseen speaking style and transcript, which can learn latent representations of the combined style embedding from multiple mel-spectrogram. Trained with ASR and feature matching, the MSG synthesizes high-diversity mel-spectrogram by controlling and mixing the individual speaking style (e.g., duration, pitch, and energy). The result also shows that the MSG synthesizes high-fidelity mel-spectrogram which has almost the same naturalness MOS score with ground-truth mel-spectrogram.
